Whisperers Code and Conduct

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure all Whisperer staff understand how to conduct themselves while performing duties.

1. Staff Conduct

1.1 Professionalism

  • Staff must remain impartial at all times.
  • Personal friendships, faction affiliations, or previous interactions must not influence decisions.
  • Staff are expected to remain calm and respectful regardless of how a player behaves.
  • Arguments with players are strictly prohibited.

1.2 Confidentiality

  • Report details are not to be shared outside the staff team.
  • Staff discussions regarding punishments, investigations, and reports must remain private.
  • Players are not entitled to know the identity of the reporting party.

1.3 Staff Characters

  • Reports must only be handled using a designated staff character.
  • Staff characters may not participate in normal roleplay activities.
  • Staff characters should remain invisible whenever possible during investigations.

Staff Visibility & Intervention Policy

Staff are not heroes, protagonists, or active participants in roleplay scenarios. Our purpose is to facilitate and protect roleplay, not become part of it.

Staff members must never intentionally reveal their staff character, staff status, or staff presence to players while handling reports or observing situations. All investigations and interventions should be conducted invisibly whenever possible.

Many disruptive players and trolls actively seek staff attention and confrontation. Direct interaction often rewards this behavior, encourages future incidents, and can create unnecessary disruption throughout the server. For this reason, visible staff intervention should be avoided whenever possible.

Even when uninvolved players are not directly participating in a situation, witnessing a staff member suddenly appear, intervene, or engage with a player can break immersion and negatively impact ongoing roleplay in the surrounding area.

The best staff actions are often the ones players never see. Our goal is to maintain a healthy roleplay environment while preserving immersion for everyone involved.

2. Responding to Reports

2.1 Report Priority

Reports should be handled in the following order:

High Priority

  • Exploiting
  • Cheating
  • Hacking
  • Duping
  • Mass RDM
  • Staff Abuse
  • Harassment
  • Serious Rule Violations

Medium Priority

  • Fail RP
  • Powergaming
  • Metagaming
  • Combat Logging
  • Exploit Abuse

Low Priority

  • Minor disputes
  • Character disagreements
  • RP quality concerns
  • Property disputes
  • Minor OOC arguments

All priorities deserve the same attention as each other. This is merely a guide for what takes priority when multiple reports are coming through at once.

2.2 Initial Response

Upon receiving a report:

  1. Acknowledge the report.
  2. Gather basic information.
  3. Determine whether immediate intervention is required.
  4. Begin an investigation before taking action on the accused player.

Staff should never punish based solely on a report claim.

3. Investigation Procedures

3.1 Observe First

Before taking action:

  • Teleport invisible, not ghosted, to the location.
  • Observe events.
  • Gather evidence.
  • Record footage whenever possible.
  • Review any clips submitted by players.

Staff should avoid becoming visible unless absolutely necessary.

3.2 Evidence Requirements

Acceptable evidence includes:

  • Staff observation.
  • Video recordings.
  • Screenshots.
  • Server logs.
  • Chat logs.
  • Voice recordings where applicable.

Punishments should never be issued solely on hearsay.

3.3 Interview Process

When necessary, interviews are to be conducted within Discord support voice channels, not in game.

  1. Speak with the reporting player.
  2. Collect any evidence they have.
  3. Compare statements against evidence.

If the evidence provides a clear rule violation by the accused, staff may take action such as a warning, kick, or ban depending on the severity of the rule broken. If the player disagrees, inform them to make a Discord ticket and communicate further in the ticket or support voice channel, not in game.

4. Roleplay First Policy

4.1 Allow RP To Resolve RP

Staff should avoid intervening when situations can be handled naturally through roleplay.

Examples:

  • Insults
  • Public disturbances
  • Disorderly conduct
  • Trespassing
  • Political disputes
  • Faction conflicts

These situations should generally be handled by:

  • Guards
  • Nobles
  • Guild leadership
  • Other in-character authorities

4.2 When Staff Should Intervene

Staff intervention becomes necessary when:

  • Rules are being broken.
  • A player has no intention to roleplay.
  • Exploits are being abused.
  • Players are harassing others.
  • OOC behavior is disrupting RP.
  • The issue cannot reasonably be resolved through RP.

Someone accidentally using IRL terms such as GG, server, LOL, AFK, or any other non-Skyrim terminology should be viewed as a slip of the tongue and should not require staff intervention unless the person is constantly doing it with disregard to the rules.

5. Punishment Guidelines

5.1 Progressive Discipline

Unless the violation is severe:

  1. Verbal Warning
  2. Written Warning
  3. Kick
  4. Temporary Ban
  5. Permanent Ban

Staff may escalate immediately for serious violations.

5.2 Consistency

Punishments must remain consistent regardless of:

  • Faction membership.
  • Rank.
  • Popularity.
  • Donator status.
  • Personal relationships.

5.3 Severe Violations

The following may result in immediate removal:

  • Hacking
  • Exploiting for personal gain
  • Duping
  • Doxxing
  • Serious harassment
  • Hate speech
  • Ban evasion
  • Staff impersonation

6. Report Documentation

Every staff action should include:

  • Date and time.
  • Staff member involved.
  • Players involved.
  • Evidence collected.
  • Rule violated.
  • Punishment issued.
  • Notes regarding the incident.

Documentation should be completed immediately after the report is closed.

7. Appeals

7.1 Appeal Rights

All punished players may appeal.

Appeals should include:

  • Character name.
  • Date of punishment.
  • Reason for punishment.
  • Explanation of events.
  • Supporting evidence.

7.2 Appeal Review

Appeals should:

  • Be reviewed by a different staff member whenever possible.
  • Be reviewed using all available evidence.
  • Remain professional and unbiased.

8. Staff Misconduct

Staff members found to be:

  • Abusing powers
  • Showing favoritism
  • Leaking staff information
  • Falsifying evidence
  • Ignoring procedures

May face:

  • Warning
  • Demotion
  • Removal from staff

Depending on severity.

Staff Reports

All staff reports are to be handled by Head of Staff only. If a player mentions they want to report a staff member, all staff must stop engaging with the person and instead direct them to the report system on Discord, where they can report the staff member directly to Head of Staff.

Do not ask who is being reported or why. Any staff found to be handling or asking questions about staff reports may also face punishment.

9. Golden Rules

The goal of staff is not to win arguments or control roleplay. The goal of staff is to protect fair, immersive roleplay while ensuring all players are treated equally under the rules.
